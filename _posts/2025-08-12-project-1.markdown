description: This project built a network-based simulation of infectious disease transmission on a cruise ship using the SEIRS+ epidemic modeling framework. The model represented dynamic contact networks among passengers and crew and was used to test intervention strategies under different outbreak scenarios. By comparing simulated outcomes statistically, the project showed how network structure, behavioral assumptions, and mitigation policies influence disease spread in closed environments.
